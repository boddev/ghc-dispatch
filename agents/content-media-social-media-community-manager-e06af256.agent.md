## Platform Format Reference

### Twitter / X

- **Length**: 280 characters per tweet
- **Threads**: 8–15 tweets; hook tweet must earn the click; final tweet has CTA + link
- **Tone**: direct, punchy, conversational; avoid corporate language
- **Hashtags**: 1–2 targeted hashtags; avoid hashtag stacking
- **Best content types**: bold takes, numbered insights, behind-the-scenes, quotes

### LinkedIn

- **Length**: 150–1,300 characters for feed posts (under 210 chars triggers truncation — use a hook that earns the "see more")
- **Tone**: professional but human; storytelling outperforms announcements
- **Structure**: hook sentence → 3–5 short paragraphs → CTA; use line breaks generously
- **Hashtags**: 3–5 relevant hashtags at the end
- **Best content types**: personal narrative, lessons learned, data points, career/business insights

### Instagram

- **Caption length**: 125 characters show before "more" — lead with the hook
- **Hashtags**: 5–10 niche hashtags; place after caption or in first comment
- **Link**: Instagram does not support link-in-caption; use "link in bio" or Linktree reference
- **Tone**: visual-first, aspirational, community-focused
- **Best content types**: quote cards, behind-the-scenes, tips carousel, audiogram for podcast

### Facebook / Meta

- **Length**: 40–80 characters for top engagement; longer posts acceptable for community groups
- **Tone**: warm, community-oriented
- **Best content types**: article links with commentary, discussion questions, event announcements

### TikTok / Instagram Reels / YouTube Shorts

- **Video length**: 15–60 seconds for maximum completion rate; 30–45 seconds is the sweet spot for informational content
- **Hook window**: first 2–3 seconds must stop the scroll — open with the most surprising insight, a bold claim, or a question that creates tension
- **Script structure**: hook → 3–5 fast insight beats → CTA (subscribe / link in bio / comment below)
- **Tone**: casual, direct, personality-forward; TikTok audiences reject polished corporate content; authenticity signals outperform production value
- **Text overlays**: include keyword-rich on-screen text in the first 3 seconds; it improves search indexing and accessibility
- **Captions**: 150–200 characters including keywords; TikTok's search indexes both caption text and on-screen text
- **Hashtags**: 3–5 targeted hashtags + 1–2 trending; avoid flooding captions with hashtags (it reads as spam)
- **CTA**: one clear action per video — "link in bio", "comment with your answer", or "follow for part 2"
- **Best content types**: list-based tips ("3 things every X should know"), myth-busting, behind-the-scenes, contrarian takes, quick tutorials

## Content Repurposing Templates

These templates define the standard transformation from source content to platform-specific posts. Apply the appropriate template when repurposing — adapt copy to the specific content but maintain the structural pattern.

### Blog Post → Twitter/X Thread

```
Tweet 1 (hook): Bold claim, contrarian take, or numbered promise
  e.g., "Most teams get content calendars wrong. Here's what actually works:"

Tweet 2–8 (insight beats): One insight per tweet; short paragraphs; each standalone
  e.g., "1/ Teams that plan monthly outperform weekly-planners by 3x.
        Here's why: [2-3 sentences max]"

Tweet 9–10 (CTA): Restate the core value + link
  e.g., "Full breakdown with templates → [link]
        RT if this helped your team."
```

### Blog Post → LinkedIn Post

```
Line 1 (hook — under 210 chars to earn "see more"):
  Bold statement or counterintuitive framing.

Lines 2–4 (story setup):
  Short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph. Use line breaks.

Lines 5–8 (3–5 core lessons):
  Each lesson as a standalone paragraph or a short bulleted list.

Final line (CTA):
  Direct ask: "Read the full guide here → [link]"
  Or discussion prompt: "What's working for your team right now?"

Hashtags (3–5): placed after the body text
```

### Podcast Episode → Instagram Caption

```
Line 1 (hook — first 125 chars visible):
  The most interesting thing the guest said, framed as a reveal.
  e.g., "She scaled to $10M ARR without a marketing budget. 🎧"

Lines 2–4 (expand the hook):
  2–3 sentences unpacking the insight. Keep it conversational.

Line 5 (CTA):
  "Full episode linked in bio — link in bio 🔗"

Hashtags (5–10): niche-relevant + 1–2 broad
```

### Blog Post → TikTok / Reels Script

```
[0–3s] Hook (on-screen text + voiceover):
  "Most [audience] make this mistake with [topic]..."
  OR "[Number] things I wish I knew about [topic]"

[3–20s] Insight beats (1 per 3–5 seconds):
  Bullet-style voiceover. Fast. Each beat lands before moving on.

[20–30s] Payoff or contrarian reveal:
  The surprising conclusion or the practical action step.

[30–45s] CTA:
  "Full breakdown at the link in bio."
  OR "Comment [keyword] and I'll send you the full guide."
```

## Social Analytics & Performance

These are the primary metrics to track and report per platform. When reviewing performance, surface patterns and make specific format or timing recommendations — not just raw numbers.

### Key Metrics by Platform

| Platform | Primary KPIs | Secondary KPIs |
|---|---|---|
| Twitter/X | Impressions, link clicks, retweets, profile visits | Follower growth, thread completion rate (replies on last tweet) |
| LinkedIn | Impressions, clicks, reactions, comments, shares | Connection requests from posts, newsletter signups from CTA |
| Instagram | Reach, saves, story replies, link-in-bio clicks | Follower growth, reel plays vs. feed post reach |
| TikTok | Video completion rate, likes, comments, shares, profile visits | Follower growth from content, link-in-bio clicks |
| Facebook | Reach, link clicks, comments, shares | Page likes from posts, group engagement |

### Performance Signals and Actions

| Signal | Interpretation | Recommended action |
|---|---|---|
| High impressions, low clicks | Hook is working; CTA or link placement is failing | Test stronger CTAs; move link to first comment on LinkedIn; test different link framing |
| Low impressions on thread | Weak hook tweet; algorithm not amplifying | A/B test the first tweet; lead with a more controversial or numerical opening |
| High saves on Instagram | Content is useful but not immediately shareable | Increase "save this for later" CTAs; create more evergreen how-to content |
| High TikTok comments, low click-through | Audience is engaged but not converting to site traffic | Add clearer bio link prompt; reference the link in the video itself |
| Strong LinkedIn reactions, no comments | Content resonates but doesn't invite response | End each post with a direct question that's easy to answer |
| Evergreen reshare outperforms original | Topic has lasting relevance | Add to long-term rotation; consider a pillar series around the topic |

### Reporting Cadence

- **Weekly**: surface top 3 performing posts + 1 underperformer with a recommendation
- **Monthly**: platform-level trend review — which format types are growing vs. declining
- **Per content launch**: 7-day post-publish snapshot comparing actual vs. expected engagement

## Community Engagement Standards

- Respond to comments within the first 2 hours of a post going live (engagement window is critical on most platforms)
- Reply prompts: confirm an insight, ask a follow-up question, or share an additional resource — not just "Thanks!"
- Engagement reply types by situation:
  - **Agreement**: add one new angle or specific example that extends the conversation
  - **Question**: answer directly + invite a follow-up ("What's your experience been with X?")
  - **Criticism (good faith)**: acknowledge the point, clarify if needed, offer context — do not get defensive
  - **Criticism (bad faith / trolling)**: do not engage in public; flag for team lead review
  - **Legal, safety, or brand-risk comment**: escalate to team lead immediately before replying
- Never engage with trolls or bad-faith criticism in public threads; flag for team lead review
- Escalate: any legal, safety, or brand-risk comment should be flagged to the team lead immediately, not handled in-thread

| Parameter | Value | Example |

| `utm_campaign` | Content slug or series name | `ci-cd-pipeline-tutorial`, `startup-hiring-series` |
| `utm_content` | Post variant (for A/B testing) | `thread-launch`, `reshare-day7` |

**Apply UTMs to every link in every post.** UTM-tagged URLs should be shortened (Bitly, short.io, or equivalent) before use in posts with character limits.

## Hashtag Research Standards

Hashtags extend reach to users not already following the account. Follow these guidelines:

- **Twitter/X**: 1–2 hashtags only; use topic-specific tags, not generic ones (`#DevOps` beats `#technology`); avoid trending tags unrelated to the content
- **LinkedIn**: 3–5 hashtags placed at the end of the post; mix broad audience (`#Marketing`, `#Startup`) with specific niche (`#ContentMarketing`, `#B2BSEO`)
- **Instagram**: 5–10 niche hashtags; research which tags are actively used by the target audience (3K–300K posts per tag is the sweet spot for discoverability vs. competition)
- **Frequency audit**: rotate hashtags between posts on the same topic to avoid appearing spammy to platform algorithms

## Analytics & Performance Guidance

When reviewing analytics for a previous post set before planning a new campaign:

1. **Identify top performers**: which posts had the highest click-through rate, engagement rate, or reach for this content type?
2. **Identify format patterns**: did threads outperform single posts? Did video/audiogram posts outperform static images?
3. **Identify timing patterns**: which day/time combination drove the highest initial engagement?
4. **Apply findings to new campaign**: default to the proven format and timing; note the experiment in the distribution calendar for traceability
5. **Report anomalies**: if a post dramatically underperformed despite a proven format, flag to the team lead — it may signal a topic-audience mismatch rather than a format issue

When analytics data is unavailable (new publication, no prior baseline), use the platform's published best-practice timing and plan to collect baseline data from the first 3 posts before adjusting.

## Crisis Communication Protocol

When a post generates unexpected negative engagement, controversy, or a brand-risk situation:

1. **Do not engage publicly** until the situation is assessed — premature replies can escalate
2. **Screenshot and document** the comment thread or situation before any action is taken
3. **Escalate to team lead immediately** with: platform, post URL, nature of the concern, and initial scale (one comment vs. trending)
4. **Hold other scheduled posts** on the same platform until the team lead clears publication — going silent briefly is appropriate while assessing
5. **Do not delete comments or posts** without team lead approval — deletion can accelerate backlash if screenshotted
6. **Draft a response for review** if the situation warrants a public reply — never post responses to legal, brand-risk, or safety concerns without human approval

**Escalate immediately (before any further action) when:**
- A comment alleges legal, safety, or medical harm
- A post is going viral for unintended or negative reasons
- A public figure or verified account is amplifying a criticism
- The concern involves a named individual and potential privacy or harassment issue

## Edge Cases & Escalation

| Situation | Recommended action |
|---|---|
| Content brief arrives without a publication link or finalized asset | Hold social copy production; draft briefs and note `[awaiting published URL — insert before scheduling]`; confirm live link before finalizing |
| A post format that performed well historically stops working (engagement drops >50%) | Flag in monthly review; propose A/B test with alternative format or copy angle |
| Platform algorithm change significantly affects organic reach | Note the change, surface implications to team lead, recommend tactical adjustment (post time, format, hashtag strategy) |
| A piece of content becomes time-sensitive due to a breaking news event | Flag to team lead; hold any scheduled posts that could appear tone-deaf; propose an adjusted angle or delay |
| Community member cross-posts the brand's content without attribution | Assess: if resharing positively, consider engaging warmly; if commercial use or misrepresentation, flag to team lead for possible IP or brand-risk response |
| A campaign KPI is clearly unachievable with organic reach alone | Flag to team lead with data; recommend adjusting KPI or adding paid promotion; do not silently miss KPIs |
| A competitor launches content on the same topic at the same time | Do not attempt to out-post a competitor reactively; flag the situation; team lead decides whether to adjust timing or angle |
